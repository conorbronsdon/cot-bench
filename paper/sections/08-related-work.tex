\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Deterministic outcome verification for agents.} tau-bench emulates
dynamic conversations between a simulated user and a tool-using agent and verifies
outcomes by comparing the final database state against an annotated goal state,
with a binary reward and no LLM judge in the outcome check; it also introduced
$\text{pass}^k$ as a reliability construct, showing frontier agents degrade sharply
as $k$ grows~\cite{taubench2024}. tau2-bench extended this with a dual-control
Dec-POMDP and a compositional task generator, and notably shipped a corrections
pass that removed incorrect expected actions, evidence that even the leading
benchmark had ground-truth defects in v1~\cite{tau2bench2025}. The Berkeley
Function Calling Leaderboard evaluates multi-turn tool use with state-based and
response-based checks that eliminate the LLM evaluator entirely, and makes
relevance and irrelevance (when \emph{not} to call a tool) first-class
metrics~\cite{bfcl2024}. COT Bench adopts state verification where a ground-truth
world exists and $\text{pass}^k$ for reliability, but departs from the
deterministic-only stance: it keeps a judge panel for the empathy and scope
dimensions that pure state checks under-measure, and labels which signal is which.

\paragraph{Test-based and contamination-aware benchmarks.} Terminal-Bench verifies
CLI tasks with per-task tests rather than judges, with deliberate difficulty
calibration that holds a performance ceiling for headroom~\cite{terminalbench2026}.
SWE-bench grades real GitHub-issue patches against hidden tests~\cite{swebench2023};
its Verified subset showed that a large fraction of nominally good tasks were
underspecified or had unfair tests, the case for an independent review
pass~\cite{swebenchverified2024}, and its eventual deprecation over training
leakage motivated held-out splits, which SWE-bench Pro answered with public,
never-released, and commercial sets~\cite{swebenchpro2025}. COT Bench's
independent-review gate and hash-pinned private holdout are these lessons in
miniature.

\paragraph{Rubric-graded conversational evaluation.} HealthBench grades 5{,}000
multi-turn health conversations against per-conversation, physician-written
rubrics, scoring each response against instance-specific criteria rather than one
generic rubric~\cite{healthbench2025}. It is the closest published precedent for
judge-graded multi-turn conversation at scale, and COT Bench adopts its core
lesson directly: every scenario carries atomic, instance-specific rubric
criteria that the judges grade as met/unmet with cited evidence, and a
criteria-bearing dimension's judge score is the weighted fraction of met
criteria (Section~\ref{sec:scoring}). The two hedges are complementary:
HealthBench's defense against judge error is rubric specificity; COT Bench
layers that same specificity on top of the deterministic state check, which
removes the judge from a third of efficacy entirely.

\paragraph{User-simulation realism.} A growing literature shows LLM-simulated
users are unreliable proxies for humans: they are over-cooperative and
stylistically uniform, and which model plays the user materially shifts measured
agent success~\cite{lostinsim2026,sim2real2026}. RealUserSim grounds simulator
personas in behavioral profiles extracted from real human--LLM conversations and
finds off-the-shelf simulators match real user style poorly~\cite{realusersim2026}.
COT Bench's response to this literature is architectural rather than cosmetic: the
simulator is removed from the success-declaration path entirely
(completion decoupling, Section~\ref{sec:design}), so simulator bias can end a
conversation early but cannot mark goals as accomplished; the residual exposure is
declared in Limitations, with behavioral simulator profiles and a sim-sensitivity
check on the roadmap.

\paragraph{LLM-as-judge methodology.} MT-Bench established the LLM-judge validation
protocol and the canonical defense (GPT-4 agreed with humans at roughly the rate
humans agree with each other) while naming position, verbosity, and self-enhancement
bias as failure modes~\cite{mtbench2023}. PoLL showed that a panel of small judges
from disjoint families beats a single strong judge on human agreement, is cheaper,
and structurally fixes self-preference because the highest positive delta for a
model occurs when it judges itself~\cite{poll2024}. Self-preference bias and its
perplexity root cause are quantified in~\cite{selfpref2024}. COT Bench's cross-lab
panel with no self-judging, its median consensus, and its published per-judge deltas
operationalize these results; the calibration protocol is the MT-Bench/PoLL
human-agreement move applied at a calibration-smoke-test scale.

\paragraph{Leaderboard statistics and length bias.} Chatbot Arena grounds rankings
in crowdsourced human preference with bootstrap confidence
intervals~\cite{chatbotarena2024}, and its governance critique, the Leaderboard
Illusion, catalogs how private-variant submission and silent deprecation distort
rankings~\cite{leaderboardillusion2025}. COT Bench has a structurally lower exposure
(fixed scenarios, no self-submitted private variants) but adopts the mapped lessons:
intervals on everything, no silent retraction, and a written model-selection and
contamination posture. Arena-Hard's separability-with-confidence is the basis for
COT Bench's rank bands~\cite{arenahard2024}. Length-Controlled AlpacaEval showed
that auto-annotators reward verbosity and that controlling for length raises
correlation with human-grounded rankings~\cite{lengthcontrolled2024}; COT Bench does
not run the full GLM debias (designed for pairwise win rates) but applies the
principle at the right altitude with a pooled length-versus-score regression.
Krippendorff's $\alpha$ is the chance-corrected agreement metric for three or more
raters on ordinal or interval labels~\cite{krippendorff2011}.

\paragraph{Anti-gaming and isolation.} The Berkeley RDI audit drove seven of eight
agentic benchmarks to near-perfect scores with a trivial agent, largely through
shared-container isolation failures and reachable answer keys, and recommended an
exploit-agent self-test~\cite{rdi2026}. A related audit catalogs task-validity and
outcome-validity failures across widely used agentic benchmarks and distills them
into an explicit rigor checklist~\cite{bestpractices2025}. COT Bench is
conversational rather than code-execution, so its isolation exposure is lower, but
it adopts the do-nothing probe directly and keeps goal and answer data out of any
surface the agent or tool simulator can read.

\paragraph{Cost-aware evaluation and human-calibrated difficulty.} The Holistic
Agent Leaderboard standardizes large-scale agent rollouts and reports
accuracy--cost Pareto frontiers, showing that one-dimensional accuracy rankings
hide most of the deployment-relevant structure: few models sit on the frontier,
and the most expensive model is rarely among them~\cite{hal2025}. BFCL likewise
publishes cost and latency alongside accuracy~\cite{bfcl2024}. COT Bench bakes
this into its headline number: the CLEAR composite weights cost and latency
directly, and the board publishes a cost--efficacy Pareto scatter with the
frontier marked rather than treating cost as a footnote. HCAST takes a complementary route to interpretable
difficulty, calibrating tasks against measured human completion
times~\cite{hcast2025}; COT Bench's easy/medium/hard difficulty labels are
author-assigned, and a human-time calibration of that axis is a natural follow-on.

\paragraph{Game-world and social-deduction evaluation.} A parallel line of work
evaluates agents in interactive game worlds rather than production domains.
Vending-Bench measures long-horizon coherence over a simulated business and finds
that breakdowns are goal-maintenance failures only weakly tied to context-window
saturation~\cite{vendingbench2025}. Werewolf Arena and WOLF use social deduction
to probe strategic communication; WOLF's central result is an asymmetry directly
relevant to evaluation design: models produce deception far more convincingly than
they detect it~\cite{werewolfarena2024,wolf2025}. TextArena and the MindGames
arena profile social and strategic skills across game portfolios with
relative-skill ratings, and document that win-rate scoring can reward robustness
to opponent error as much as genuine skill~\cite{textarena2025,mindgames2026}.
COT Bench draws two lessons from this genre without adopting its scoring. First,
if strong models detect deception barely above chance, a transcript-reading judge
is a weak last line of defense, which is the case for grading the world
deterministically wherever ground truth exists. Second, the genre's coherence
findings imply that goal maintenance under perturbation is measurable within a
short horizon; COT Bench's ten-turn sessions measure within-conversation
reliability and recovery, not long-horizon autonomy, and a recovery-probe scenario
tier is planned (Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).

\paragraph{The CLEAR framework and the Galileo lineage.} COT Bench aligns its
metric framework with CLEAR, which reported that accuracy-only evaluation correlates
about 0.41 with production success while multi-dimensional evaluation reaches about
0.83~\cite{clear2025}. Its evaluation methodology draws on Galileo's open
agent-leaderboard~\cite{galileo2025}.

\paragraph{Summary of position.} No single element of COT Bench is novel in
isolation: state grading is tau-bench's and BFCL's, $\text{pass}^k$ is
tau-bench's, the judge panel is PoLL's, the holdout is SWE-bench Pro's, the
intervals and bands are Chatbot Arena's and Arena-Hard's, and the do-nothing probe
is the RDI audit's. The contribution is the combination, which to our knowledge no
benchmark above assembles: multi-turn user simulation with the simulator removed
from the success-declaration path, deterministic state verification against
per-scenario ground-truth worlds, a cross-lab judge panel with published per-judge
scores and chance-corrected agreement, and pre-registration governance with a
hash-pinned private holdout, applied to production conversational domains where
neither deterministic-only nor judge-only evaluation is honest on its own.
