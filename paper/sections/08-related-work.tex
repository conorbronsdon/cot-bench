\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Deterministic outcome verification for agents.} tau-bench emulates
dynamic conversations between a simulated user and a tool-using agent and verifies
outcomes by comparing the final database state against an annotated goal state,
with a binary reward and no LLM judge in the outcome check; it also introduced
$\text{pass}^k$ as a reliability construct, showing frontier agents degrade sharply
as $k$ grows~\cite{taubench2024}. tau2-bench extended this with a dual-control
Dec-POMDP and a compositional task generator, and notably shipped a corrections
pass that removed incorrect expected actions, evidence that even the leading
benchmark had ground-truth defects in v1~\cite{tau2bench2025}. The Berkeley
Function Calling Leaderboard evaluates multi-turn tool use with state-based and
response-based checks that eliminate the LLM evaluator entirely, and makes
relevance and irrelevance (when \emph{not} to call a tool) first-class
metrics~\cite{bfcl2024}. COT Bench adopts state verification where a ground-truth
world exists and $\text{pass}^k$ for reliability, but departs from the
deterministic-only stance: it keeps a judge panel for the empathy and scope
dimensions that pure state checks under-measure, and labels which signal is which.

\paragraph{Test-based and contamination-aware benchmarks.} Terminal-Bench verifies
CLI tasks with per-task tests rather than judges, with deliberate difficulty
calibration that holds a performance ceiling for headroom~\cite{terminalbench2026}.
SWE-bench grades real GitHub-issue patches against hidden tests~\cite{swebench2023};
its Verified subset showed that a large fraction of nominally good tasks were
underspecified or had unfair tests, the case for an independent review
pass~\cite{swebenchverified2024}, and its eventual deprecation over training
leakage motivated held-out splits, which SWE-bench Pro answered with public,
never-released, and commercial sets~\cite{swebenchpro2025}. COT Bench's
independent-review gate and hash-pinned private holdout are these lessons in
miniature.

\paragraph{LLM-as-judge methodology.} MT-Bench established the LLM-judge validation
protocol and the canonical defense (GPT-4 agreed with humans at roughly the rate
humans agree with each other) while naming position, verbosity, and self-enhancement
bias as failure modes~\cite{mtbench2023}. PoLL showed that a panel of small judges
from disjoint families beats a single strong judge on human agreement, is cheaper,
and structurally fixes self-preference because the highest positive delta for a
model occurs when it judges itself~\cite{poll2024}. Self-preference bias and its
perplexity root cause are quantified in~\cite{selfpref2024}. COT Bench's cross-lab
panel with no self-judging, its median consensus, and its published per-judge deltas
operationalize these results; the calibration protocol is the MT-Bench/PoLL
human-agreement move applied at a calibration-smoke-test scale.

\paragraph{Leaderboard statistics and length bias.} Chatbot Arena grounds rankings
in crowdsourced human preference with bootstrap confidence
intervals~\cite{chatbotarena2024}, and its governance critique, the Leaderboard
Illusion, catalogs how private-variant submission and silent deprecation distort
rankings~\cite{leaderboardillusion2025}. COT Bench has a structurally lower exposure
(fixed scenarios, no self-submitted private variants) but adopts the mapped lessons:
intervals on everything, no silent retraction, and a written model-selection and
contamination posture. Arena-Hard's separability-with-confidence is the basis for
COT Bench's rank bands~\cite{arenahard2024}. Length-Controlled AlpacaEval showed
that auto-annotators reward verbosity and that controlling for length raises
correlation with human-grounded rankings~\cite{lengthcontrolled2024}; COT Bench does
not run the full GLM debias (designed for pairwise win rates) but applies the
principle at the right altitude with a pooled length-versus-score regression.
Krippendorff's $\alpha$ is the chance-corrected agreement metric for three or more
raters on ordinal or interval labels~\cite{krippendorff2011}.

\paragraph{Anti-gaming and isolation.} The Berkeley RDI audit drove seven of eight
agentic benchmarks to near-perfect scores with a trivial agent, largely through
shared-container isolation failures and reachable answer keys, and recommended an
exploit-agent self-test~\cite{rdi2026}. COT Bench is conversational rather than
code-execution, so its isolation exposure is lower, but it adopts the do-nothing
probe directly and keeps goal and answer data out of any surface the agent or tool
simulator can read.

\paragraph{The CLEAR framework and the Galileo lineage.} COT Bench aligns its
metric framework with CLEAR, which reported that accuracy-only evaluation correlates
about 0.41 with production success while multi-dimensional evaluation reaches about
0.83~\cite{clear2025}. Its evaluation methodology draws on Galileo's open
agent-leaderboard~\cite{galileo2025}.
